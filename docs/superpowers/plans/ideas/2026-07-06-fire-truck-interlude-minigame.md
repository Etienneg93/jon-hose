# Fire Truck Interlude — Between-Worlds Minigame (raw idea)

**Date:** 2026-07-06 · **Status: CONCEPT ONLY — no spec yet.** Captured on the
user's word ("just keep this as an idea for now, we'll write specs for it
later"). Do not build until it's spec'd.

## The pitch

A between-worlds interstitial: Jon is forced into a high-speed **fire truck
chase/escape**. Something kicks it off (chase OR escape — both on the table).
Jon is either hanging off the truck or riding inside it. You drive the truck as
it blazes down the road, and its **super-powerful mounted hose** is your weapon —
blast obstacles out of the way and gun down otherwise-brutal enemies while the
world zooms past at "high speed" (fast-scrolling background sells the speed).

Reference feel: the **Battletoads Turbo Tunnel / high-speed vehicle stage** —
the on-rails speed-dodging fantasy — **but deliberately NOT as punishing.** A
palate-cleanser set-piece, not a rage-quit wall.

## Why it fits (first-glance, not a design)

- Reuses the game's core verb — **the hose is still the weapon** — in a new
  frame, so it reads as "same Jon, new situation" rather than a random genre
  swap.
- Natural home is a **world transition** (the "between worlds" beat), matching
  the elemental-altar / act-boundary structure already in the vision.

## Deliberately unresolved (for the eventual spec)

- Chase vs. escape (or both, per instance) — and what triggers it.
- Jon on the truck vs. inside it — affects framing, aim, and vulnerability.
- On-rails auto-scroll vs. player-driven throttle/lane control.
- What "obstacles" and "super-hard enemies" are, and how hose aim maps to a
  side/forward-scrolling frame (vs. the top-down main game).
- Fail state & stakes — how forgiving, and what a wipe costs.
- Whether it's a scored one-off, a recurring interlude, or tied to progression.

## Constraints to honor when this graduates to a spec

- **No jump, no melee** (hard cut) — the hose and steering are the whole
  toolkit.
- Rim-is-hitbox / one-shape-draw-and-hit contract still applies to any new
  telegraphed hazard.
- Minimal placeholder-art dependency; hand-cleaned sprites are off-limits to
  bakers (see CLAUDE.md art rules) — a new frame likely needs its own asset
  plan.
